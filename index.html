Hacker who breached Telegana police app

A 20-year-old student from Greater Noida, Jatin Kumar, has been arrested for hacking into the Telangana police’s HawkEye and TSCOP applications. He posted details of the breach on a hacker forum, databreachforum.st, and offered the compromised data for sale at $150. The hacker, who goes by the handle “Adm1nFr1end”, also provided his Telegram IDs for potential buyers to contact him.
The Telangana police’s Director-General, Ravi Gupta, confirmed the arrest and stated that the hacker had not deleted or made the data unusable, and that the breach did not result in data loss. The police have also initiated a comprehensive security audit of all their applications and websites to prevent future breaches.



Police denies collection of private data, says password was weak

Telangana DGP Ravi Gupta also stated that the police’s Hawkeye mobile application only retains user information such as mobile numbers, addresses, and email IDs as part of its data repository. “Prima-facie, it is suspected that because of a weak / compromised password, the intruder might have obtained access to certain segments of Hawkeye data by generating a report,” he said in a release.
About the TSCOP app, the Telangana DGP said it is utilized for in-house tasks, “guaranteeing no collection of confidential/financial user data”. He also categorically denied Kodali’s statement that the police is collecting information of people checking into hotels. “It is a fact that TSCOP does not collect any Visitor / Hotel Management Data, at all. Hence, it is absolutely incorrect to say that TSCOP pushed / gave such Data to any third party. Therefore, certain related media reports that appeared in newspapers are denied,” he added.





Hyderabad: Ahead of Bakrid, HC calls for curb on illegal animal slaughter

Hyderabad: With less than a week left for Bakrid, which will be celebrated in Hyderabad and other parts of the country on June 17, the Telangana High Court has called for measures to curb illegal animal slaughter.

The emphasis was made during the hearing of a petition filed by the Gau Gyan Foundation.

The petition has sought directives to ensure that laws related to animal transportation and slaughter are properly implemented.
